# Forum Posts — Phase 0 Discovery

Goal: surface marketing agencies (15–50 people) who feel the "information scattered across tools" pain, without pitching a product. Get them talking about their last bad week.

**Mom Test rules — non-negotiable:**
- Ask about their life, not your idea
- Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about the future
- Talk less, listen more
- No "would you" / "could you" / "do you think" questions

**Conversion goal:** DM → 20-min call (run [INTERVIEW_SCRIPT.md](INTERVIEW_SCRIPT.md))

**Tracking:** for every post, log in a spreadsheet — platform, date, link, upvotes, comments, DMs received, calls booked. The signal is *DMs and calls*, not upvotes.

> Not selling anything, trying to figure out if this is just my problem or a "everyone deals with this" problem.

## DM playbook (when someone replies/DMs)

If they engage with a story, reply:

> Thanks for sharing — that's exactly the kind of thing I'm trying to understand. Would you be open to a 20-min call this or next week? I'll ask about a few specific moments and what they cost. No pitch — I'm not selling anything yet, just learning. Times that work for you?

If they engage but don't have time:

> Totally get it. One async question if you have 2 mins: when the [specific thing they mentioned] happened, who was the first person to realize something had gone wrong, and how did they realize it? Just curious about the "discovery moment."

## Posting cadence

- Don't blast all six in one week — looks like spam, and you can't process the responses.
- One post every 2–3 days, rotate platforms.
- Watch one post for 48 hours before posting the next variant — read the comments, refine the next post's framing based on what resonated.
- Aim: **10 conversations in 4 weeks** (the Phase 0 → Phase 1 gate is 4 of 7 confirming pain — give yourself slack on top).

---

## What "demand signal" looks like

Strong:
- Unprompted comments like "we deal with this every week"
- DMs asking "are you building something?"
- People tagging colleagues
- Calls booked

Weak:
- Lots of upvotes, no DMs (people sympathize, don't feel pain)
- Comments saying "just use Notion better" (their pain is shaped differently)
- Crickets (post framing is off — rewrite, retry on different platform)
